Technology and AI Pathway

Technology and AI careers are not only about coding. They require problem-solving, digital confidence, ethical thinking and the ability to keep learning as tools change. This roadmap shows youths what to build first and how to start taking action.

← Back to Skills Roadmap Explore Careers

Why this sector matters

In a technology-driven economy, many jobs are being changed by automation, artificial intelligence, data systems and digital platforms. Youths who understand how technology works will be better prepared to adapt, even if their future job is not directly a technology job.

This pathway helps users reduce skills mismatch by showing the actual skills needed in technology-related careers, instead of only giving vague advice like “learn coding”.

Pathway Goal

Build the foundation to understand digital tools, solve problems using technology, work with data and use AI responsibly in school, projects and future workplaces.

Beginner Roadmap

A simple step-by-step pathway for youths who want to start building technology and AI skills.

1

Build digital basics

Start with typing, file organisation, online research, spreadsheets and basic presentation tools.

2

Learn simple coding

Try HTML, CSS or Python. Focus on understanding logic before memorising complicated syntax.

3

Create a small project

Build a website, quiz, calculator, chatbot idea, data chart or automation that solves a simple problem.

4

Apply AI responsibly

Use AI to brainstorm, summarise or test ideas, while checking accuracy and avoiding over-reliance.

Suggested Actions for Youths

These actions make the pathway practical. Instead of telling users to “upskill”, the page gives them clear ways to begin.

What to do first

  • Complete a beginner coding lesson using Python, HTML or JavaScript.
  • Use spreadsheets to organise survey results and create simple charts.
  • Build a small website or digital prototype linked to a real problem.
  • Try AI tools, but always fact-check the output before using it.
  • Join STEM, robotics, coding, design or entrepreneurship activities in school.
